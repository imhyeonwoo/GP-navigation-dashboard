Convention

The streamed quaternion is interpreted as the FRD body attitude matrix C_b_to_n. The displayed body frame therefore follows b: +x forward, +y right, +z down.

The aircraft mesh intentionally stays in the mounted IMU FLU convention, so the vehicle is not flipped upside-down just to match the formal body-frame sign convention. The bright wingtip marker shows +i_y / left.

The optional IMU sensor frame is derived with C_IMU_to_b = diag(1,-1,-1).

The IMU attitude is computed as C_imu_to_n = C_b_to_n * C_IMU_to_b, where C_IMU_to_b^T = C_IMU_to_b.

The roll, pitch, yaw cards are the derived b-frame ZYX yaw-pitch-roll values, and yaw is shown as a signed angle in [-180, 180].

Each rendered frame has explicit axis labels. Use the coordinate-frame toggles to hide any frame you do not want to see. The aircraft mesh can also be toggled separately when you want to inspect only the axes.

Left drag: orbit camera. Right drag: pan the 3D view in space. Wheel: zoom.
